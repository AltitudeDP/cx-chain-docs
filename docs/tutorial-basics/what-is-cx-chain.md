# What is CX Chain?

CX Chain is a **decentralized game execution layer** designed to revolutionize on-chain gaming by democratizing liquidity and enabling permissionless game operations. Built as a **purpose-specific Layer 1 on Avalanche**, CX Chain integrates game economies with automated liquidity management and verifiable randomness, ensuring fairness and transparency in gameplay. On CX Chain, games are **permissionless**, yield is **native**, and fairness is **verifiable**.

#### What Makes CX Chain Different

*   **Purpose-Built Architecture**: Unlike generic blockchains, CX Chain directly integrates game economies with automated liquidity management and verifiable and audited randomness. This specialized design aims to provide a sustainable Web3 economy, not merely another tech layer.
*   **Automated Payouts**: Smart contracts on CX Chain handle **fast and fair payments** with no middlemen.
*   **Decentralized Liquidity Pool**: Any participant can act as a liquidity provider or game operator. Anyone can be the "house" by **delegating assets to earn rewards**, promoting a sustainable Web3 economy. Revenue is split between node holders and delegators.
*   **Community-Based Ownership**: The platform fosters a community-driven gaming ecosystem, where revenue is shared between node holders and delegators, aligning incentives across the network.
*   **Scalable Game Launch**: CX Chain has partnered with Hypha to make staking and delegation easy, supporting scalable game launches.

#### What You Can Do on CX Chain

*   **Play Games**: Experience games where you truly own your items and can trade them with other players, with five games currently in development. Players can gamble with CX tokens in exclusive games, tournaments, and rake-free games.
*   **Earn Yield**: Participate in financial services that offer returns on your digital assets through lending and liquidity provision. You can delegate assets to a House Node to earn yield from the gameplay economy.
*   **Become a Node Operator**: Purchase an **NFT-based House Node License** to contribute to network security, validate transactions, and ensure game fairness. Node operators earn fees from games and smart contract executions running on CX Chain.
*   **Provide House Capital**: **Stake CX tokens into liquidity pools** to provide house capital for games and earn a share of gameplay revenue.
*   **Influence Governance**: Use **CX Tokens for governance**, voting or delegating votes on new games, smart contracts, and protocol upgrades. This design prevents token accumulation from skewing protocol control.
*   **Gain Exclusive Access**: Holding or staking CX tokens can unlock **exclusive games, rake rebates (Tier 1-3 for 10-30%), and rake-free gameplay options**.
*   **Publish Games**: The CX Token is required as **collateral for game publishing** and access to infrastructure. Slashing penalties apply to game publishers upon protocol violations.

#### Key Concepts

*   **CX Token (CX)**: This is the **native utility token** on CX Chain with a **fixed total supply of 1,000,000,000 CX**. It's used for governance, staking into liquidity pools, transaction fees (partially **flared/burned** to control supply), collateral, and optional player utility. There is no fixed inflation rate.
*   **House Node Licenses (NFTs)**: These **NFT-based licenses** grant the right to validate transactions, ensure game fairness, and secure the network. Validators require these licenses, which are **capped at 1,000 total**. License holders can stake them into physical validator nodes to contribute EVM compute and receive protocol-level rewards.
*   **Validators**: These are the House Nodes that process transactions and secure the network.
*   **Smart Contracts**: Programs that run on CX Chain, handling automated payouts, enforcing vesting schedules and token release mechanisms, and powering everything from simple transfers to complex applications.
*   **Delegators**: Participants who back NFT House Nodes by locking assets; they must hold a minimum amount of CX Tokens as collateral (e.g., 10% of delegated value). They earn yield from delegated assets.

#### How CX Chain Works (Simply)

1.  **Purchase a License**: Become a **node operator** by acquiring a House License. Batch 1 of the public License Sale has been successfully completed, with 250 nodes sold out.
2.  **Stake to Validate**: Run your own hardware or use a preferred partner like Hypha to **stake your House License and validate transactions**, contributing to network security.
3.  **Accumulate & Withdraw**: Earn rewards for securing the network and processing transactions.
4.  **Delegate Assets**: Other players can interact directly through smart contracts, and asset holders can **delegate their assets to a House Node** to earn yield on games.

#### Common Questions

*   **Is CX Chain secure?**
 Yes. CX Chain is a decentralized game execution layer built on Avalanche, secured by NFT-based House Node Licenses. Game publishers and nodes are subject to **slashing penalties** for malicious acts, protocol violations, or failure to meet operational standards. Slashed tokens may be burned or redistributed to maintain economic security.
*   **Do I need technical knowledge?** While becoming a node operator involves running hardware or using a partner like Hypha, the overall goal is to democratize liquidity. The platform is designed for participants to interact directly through smart contracts.
*   **What if I make a mistake?** Game publishers and nodes are subject to **slashing penalties** for malicious acts, protocol violations, or failure to meet operational standards. Slashing collateral is held in CX Tokens. Additionally, delegators backing NFT House Nodes are required to hold a minimum amount of CX Tokens as collateral.
*   **How is this different from generic blockchains?** CX Chain is a **purpose-built Layer 1** specifically designed for on-chain gaming, directly integrating game economies with automated liquidity management and verifiable randomness. It prioritizes features like automated payouts, decentralized liquidity pools, and community-based ownership for a gaming ecosystem, unlike generic blockchains.
