# Introduction to CX Chain Tokens

This document introduces the core digital assets fundamental to the CX Chain ecosystem. The network centers around two primary digital assets: the CX Token (CX) and the CX Node License (NFT).

#### Understanding Digital Assets on CX Chain

*   **CX Token (CX)**: This is the **native utility token** of CX Chain. It has a **fixed and capped total supply of 1,000,000,000 CX**. The token's emission is governed by network gas usage fees, with **no fixed inflation rate**. Instead, a portion of transaction fees paid in CX is "flared" (burned) to dynamically reduce supply.
*   **CX Node License (NFT)**: These are **NFT-based licenses** that grant the right to **validate transactions, ensure game fairness, and secure the network**. There is a **total cap of 1,000 NFT Licenses**. A key distinction is that **no token staking is required for validator eligibility**; instead, these NFT licenses determine who can act as a validator. Batch 1 of these licenses, totaling 250 nodes, has already sold out.

#### Key Utilities and Functions of CX Chain Digital Assets

**CX Token (CX)** serves several critical functions within the CX Chain ecosystem:

*   **Governance**: The CX Token is the **governance vehicle**, allowing community members to **vote or delegate votes** on new game additions, smart contract approvals, and protocol upgrades.
*   **Liquidity Provision**: CX Tokens can be **staked into decentralized liquidity pools** to provide "house capital" for games, allowing participants to **earn a share of gameplay revenue**.
*   **Transaction Fees**: CX is used to pay for **gas and other on-chain operations**. A portion of these fees is **flared (burned)**, contributing to the token's dynamic supply control. Notably, gamblers do not pay transaction fees, but stakers, delegators, and game developers do.
*   **Collateral & Access**: CX Tokens are **required for game publishing** and to **access infrastructure**. Game publishers must also hold **slashing collateral in CX Tokens** to deter malicious behavior.
*   **Optional Player Utility**: Holding or staking CX Tokens can unlock **exclusive games, provide rake rebates, and offer rake-free gameplay options**. Players also have the option to gamble directly with CX tokens.

**CX Node License (NFT)** provides the following capabilities:

*   **Network Security and Validation**: Holders of these NFTs act as **validators (House Nodes)**, processing transactions and securing the network. They can be staked into physical validator nodes to receive protocol-level rewards.
*   **Earning Protocol Fees**: House Nodes **earn protocol fees and yield from delegated assets**. They also earn fees directly from games and smart contract executions.
*   **Delegation Model**: Delegators can back NFT House Nodes by locking their assets. For instance, they must hold a minimum amount of CX Tokens as collateral, such as 10% of their delegated value.

The **Ownership Structure** further clarifies this:
*   An **Asset Holder** can buy CX Tokens and delegate them to a House Node.
*   A **House License Holder** buys a House License, stakes it, receives asset delegations, and stakes those assets to the CX Pool to earn yield from games.

#### CX Chain Tokenomics and Supply Dynamics

The total supply of CX Token is **fixed at 1 billion CX at genesis**, meaning there is **no fixed inflation**. Instead, token emission is directly **governed by network activity**. Transaction fees generate gas costs paid in CX, a portion of which is **flared (burned)** to reduce the supply dynamically. The platform may also implement **buyback mechanisms via treasury revenue** to further contribute to token scarcity. Token redistribution primarily occurs through gameplay rewards and node license airdrops.

#### Security and Vesting Enforcement

To ensure the integrity and longevity of the CX Chain ecosystem:

*   **Slashing & Security**: Both game publishers and nodes are subject to **slashing penalties**. This collateral, held in CX Tokens, is triggered by malicious acts or protocol violations. Slashed tokens may be burned or redistributed to maintain economic security.
*   **Vesting Enforcement**: All vesting schedules and token release mechanisms for stakeholders are **enforced via smart contracts**. This ensures transparency, with cliff periods and linear vesting designed to reduce sell pressure and align incentives with the project's long-term health.
